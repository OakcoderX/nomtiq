Restaurant recommendation use case

An AI family-friendly restaurant finder for the whole table

“Family-friendly” is not a single label. A useful choice must work for the youngest child, the oldest guest, the strictest food constraint, and the people getting everyone there.

Describe the family, not just the cuisine

Beijing

北京三代人家庭聚餐，8 个人，有一位老人和两个小孩。想吃中餐，人均 200 元左右，少走路，最好有电梯或无障碍入口。


English

Find a family-friendly restaurant near our hotel for four adults and two children. One guest has a peanut allergy, we need stroller access, and dinner must finish before 7:30.

Age alone does not reveal what the party needs. The agent should clarify the requirements that can eliminate a restaurant before searching.

Children and seating

Ask whether the group needs high chairs, stroller space, a changing area, simple food, or a quick first course. “Good for kids” in a listing does not prove any specific facility.

Older guests and access

Walking distance, stairs, elevators, drop-off points, seating comfort, restroom access, and noise can outweigh novelty. Treat accessibility claims as facts that need current support.

Allergies and hard food needs

Separate allergies and medical restrictions from preferences. A menu mention is not proof against cross-contact; serious allergy accommodation should be confirmed directly with the restaurant.

Shared-table fit

Party size, table format, portion style, spice tolerance, vegetarian choices, and whether dishes are easy to share all change how well a restaurant works for a mixed group.

Travel, timing, and budget

Clarify whether the family is walking, driving, or taking transit; whether the budget is per person or total; and whether young children or older guests impose a firm meal window. The shortest map distance is not always the easiest arrival.

What the 2+1 shortlist should explain

  • Safest fit: the strongest match for hard food needs, access, party size, timing, and budget.
  • Alternative fit: another supported choice with a meaningful tradeoff, such as easier arrival versus a broader menu.
  • Exploration choice: a more distinctive option, clearly labeled when family facilities or another important detail remain uncertain.

Each result should identify who in the family it serves well, the possible friction point, and the details that require confirmation. A generic “family-friendly” tag is not enough evidence.

Facts Nomtiq must not invent

High chairs, stroller access, elevators, accessible restrooms, allergen handling, quiet seating, parking, private rooms, current prices, opening hours, and table availability can change. Nomtiq reports live support and uncertainty; it does not contact the venue or make a reservation.
